Freight 101

Basic Freight Terms

Welcome to Freight 101! This module introduces key freight shipping terms.

  • Freight: Goods transported in bulk by truck, ship, train, or plane.
  • Shipper: The company or person sending the freight.
  • Carrier: The company that physically moves the freight (like trucking companies).
  • 3PL (Third-Party Logistics): Companies that manage shipping on behalf of shippers.
  • LTL (Less Than Truckload): Freight shipments smaller than a full truckload, usually combined with other loads.
  • FTL (Full Truckload): A shipment that fills an entire truck.
  • Pallet: A flat transport structure that supports goods for handling and storage.
  • Freight Class: A standardized category affecting shipping rates based on density, value, and handling.
  • Bill of Lading (BOL): A legal document between shipper and carrier detailing shipment specifics.
  • Tracking: Monitoring the shipment's progress until delivery.
  • Invoice: Billing document issued after shipment delivery.
